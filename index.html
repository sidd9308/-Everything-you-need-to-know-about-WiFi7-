Everything you need to know about WiFi7

Siddharth | Dec 30 2023 - 7:50 am PT
0 Comments

 Wi-Fi 7, the upcoming standard in internet connectivity, is on the verge of entering the Indian market, bringing a significant technological leap. This revolutionary advancement promises swifter connections, reduced latency, and the capability to handle more connections compared to its predecessors. The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) in India is contemplating freeing up the lower band of the 6GHz spectrum for Wi-Fi 7 usage, enabling individuals to upgrade to routers that deliver ultra-high-quality content at speeds five times faster than those of Wi-Fi 6E routers. Wi-Fi 7, also recognized as IEEE 802.11be, operates across 2.5GHz, 5GHz, and 6GHz bands, heralding numerous benefits for the interconnected world.

Discussions surrounding Wi-Fi 7, the imminent advancement in internet connectivity, have been ongoing for a while, anticipating its arrival in the Indian market. The populace is gearing up to experience this cutting-edge, high-speed connectivity as supported devices prepare to enter the market soon.

For users in India, here's a concise overview of how this technology will elevate their experience. Wi-Fi 7 assures faster connections, diminished latency, and enhanced capacity for supported devices to manage multiple connections, surpassing the capabilities of prior standards like Wi-Fi 6 and the more capable Wi-Fi 6E, released in 2019 and 2021, respectively.

The Government's involvement in Wi-Fi 7's implementation in India is under consideration. Reports indicate that the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) is contemplating freeing up the lower band of the 6GHz spectrum for Wi-Fi connectivity. This prospective move will empower individuals to upgrade to Wi-Fi 7 routers, facilitating access to ultra-high-quality content at speeds reported to be five times faster than those attainable with current Wi-Fi 6E-compatible routers.

Is Wi-Fi 7 an immediate necessity? Perhaps not, but the escalating demand for faster and more reliable wireless connectivity is evident as cloud-based services gain prominence. Operating across 2.5GHz, 5GHz, and 6GHz bands, Wi-Fi 7, also known as IEEE 802.11be, is poised to introduce a series of advantages to our increasingly interconnected world.

Notable industry leaders, like NETGEAR, are enthusiastic about seizing the opportunity to introduce this technology in the Indian market. Marthesh Nagendra, Sales Director for India MEA South-East Asia Region at NETGEAR, highlighted, "This revolutionary technology has a competitive edge over existing technologies like Wi-Fi 6E and Wi-Fi 6. The latest Wi-Fi 7 technology represents a significant leap forward in wireless technology, addressing the pressing needs of our hyper-connected world."

Wi-Fi 7, with its benefits for users, is projected to offer wireless data transfer speeds of 46Gbps, a substantial advancement five times faster than Wi-Fi 6E routers. This will facilitate handling bandwidth-intensive applications, enabling smooth high-definition video streaming over internet connections. The doubled bandwidth channels up to 320MHz will accommodate more devices at high speeds, ensuring efficiency by reducing network congestion in densely populated environments.

Moreover, Wi-Fi 7 significantly minimizes latency, offering real-time experiences for online gaming, video conferencing, and streaming. Its utilization of both 5GHz and 6GHz bands for simultaneous data transmission and reception ensures more reliable connections for users.
